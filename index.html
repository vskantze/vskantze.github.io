Selected Publications

Identification of metabotypes in complex biological data using tensor decomposition
Skantze V, Wallman M, Sandberg AS, Landberg R, Jirstrand M, Brunius C (2023), Chemometrics and Intelligent Laboratory Systems
DOI: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.chemolab.2023.104733
This paper presents tensor decomposition methods for identifying distinct metabolic response types ("metabotypes") in complex biological datasets, facilitating precision nutrition approaches.
Data-driven analysis and prediction of dynamic postprandial metabolic response to multiple dietary challenges using dynamic mode decomposition
Skantze V, Jirstrand M, Brunius C, Sandberg AS, Landberg R, Wallman M (2024), Frontiers in Nutrition
DOI: https://doi.org/10.3389/fnut.2023.1304540
This study uses dynamic mode decomposition—a data-driven method—to model and predict individual metabolic responses (“postprandial”) to various dietary challenges. It provides insights into personalizing nutritional interventions.
Differential Responders to a Mixed Meal Tolerance Test Associated with Type 2 Diabetes Risk Factors and Gut Microbiota — Data from the MEDGI-Carb Randomized Controlled Trial
Skantze V, Hjorth T, Wallman M, Brunius C, Dicksved J, Pelve EA, et al. (2023), Nutrients
DOI: https://doi.org/10.3390/nu15204369
Investigates how responses to mixed meal tests vary and are linked to clinical risk factors and gut microbiota composition, advancing understanding of type 2 diabetes risk prediction and personalized nutrition.
Identifying metabotypes from complex biological data using PARAFAC

DOI: https://doi.org/10.1093/cdn/nzab063
